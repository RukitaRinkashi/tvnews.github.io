Undersecretary Impersonator Exploits Trust for Illicit Gain in Chinese Business Community

By - Jasmine Cruz March, 2024 Comments (3) 5 Mins Read
Share Post:
Email : 126k 12k

A disturbing pattern has emerged within the Chinese business community, where an individual named Johndel Costales a.k.a. CJ Costales pretending as a government official has been leveraging fabricated connections to exploit trust and extort money from unsuspecting Chinese entrepreneurs. The imposter, claiming to hold a high-ranking position as the Undersecretary for Political Affairs, has been using falsified photographs with government officials, including generals, mayors, governors, and even the President and First Lady, to deceive his victims.

Under the guise of offering protection and assistance, Costales gains the trust of store managers and its employees by weaving elaborate tales of influence and political power. Once ingratiated into the inner circles of these establishments, he manipulates his newfound relationships to coerce introductions to other Chinese business owners, promising assistance in times of trouble in their business in the country.

However, Costales' facade of benevolence quickly crumbles as he shifts to his true agenda and it is demanding money under the guise of assisting the said Chinese business owners. Costales exploits his victims' generosity and goodwill, shamelessly soliciting funds.

On an interview with one of the victims, who requested not to divulge his identity to the public said, "As a member of the Chinese community, I condemn these fraudulent actions. We must remain vigilant and protect each other from such scams."

Undersecretary Impersonator

This event underscores the significance of due diligence in verifying claims of and reinforcing the need for transparency and accountability in all aspects of governance to avert intentions like Costales', in pursuit to juice off the unaware with his pretensions.
